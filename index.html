Why it matters

Forests regulate climate, support biodiversity, reduce erosion and filter water. Conserving water protects drinking supplies, preserves freshwater habitats, and reduces energy used for treatment and transport. Together they keep ecosystems resilient.

Protect Forests

Simple actions

  • Plant native trees and maintain them.
  • Support sustainable wood and paper products (look for certified labels).
  • Protect old growth and avoid clearing forests for short-term gain.

Community impact

Support local conservation groups, volunteer for tree-planting events, and advocate for parks and protected areas in local planning processes.

Save Water

At home

  • Fix leaks promptly — a single drip can waste gallons per day.
  • Install low-flow showerheads and efficient appliances.
  • Collect rainwater for irrigation where legal.

In the community

Support green infrastructure (rain gardens, permeable pavements) and policies that reduce runoff and protect rivers and wetlands.

How to get involved

Volunteer

Join local cleanup days, tree planting, or citizen science projects that monitor water quality.

Educate

Share tips with friends and schools — small behavior changes scale up when communities adopt them.

Advocate

Write to local officials asking for protected areas, water-wise landscaping in public spaces, and investment in wastewater improvements.

Donate

Support trusted nonprofits working on reforestation, watershed protection, and environmental education.
